Imagery Analysis

Surface reflectance of various land types

I conducted a comprehensive study on land cover types using the Landsat 8 OLI image spanning from Lake Scugog to Bewdley in Southern Ontario. By recording the reflectance values across Bands 1 to 7 during the summer of 2019, seven distinct land types were precisely characterized. The resulting graph vividly illustrates the unique spectral behaviors exhibited by each land type, providing invaluable information for accurate identification, classification, and monitoring purposes.

Image Filtering

Filtered satellite imagery

Image filtering is a key technique in digital image processing used to enhance, modify, or extract information by applying mathematical operations to individual pixels. It helps highlight features, reduce noise, or sharpen image details.

In an analysis of SPOT panchromatic imagery from Stouffville, I examined the effects of high-pass filtering by comparing the spectral profiles of the original image and its 3×3 and 7×7 filtered versions. This comparison illustrated how high-pass filtering altered spectral characteristics, enhancing specific features for more targeted analysis and interpretation.
